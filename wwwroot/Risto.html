Yield: 20 Serving: 10 Prep time: 20 m Cook time: 40 m Ready in: 55 m
Cuisine: Italian Course: Lunch Skill Level: Medium
⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ (4.5/ 5)

Mushroom Risotto is a creamy, elegant Italian rice dish made by slowly cooking arborio rice with warm stock, butter, and aromatics, then blending it with tender, marinated mushrooms sautéed with herbs. The dish is finished with parmesan cheese and fresh herbs, offering a rich, savory, and slightly tangy flavor profile. Perfect for cozy dinners or special occasions, it balances creaminess with the earthy depth of mushrooms and the freshness of herbs.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup arborio rice (or short-grain rice)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp butter
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken stock (kept warm)
  • 1 small onion (finely chopped)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)
  • 200g mushrooms (button, cremini, or mixed mushrooms — sliced)
  • 1/2 tsp dried thyme or fresh thyme leaves
  • A pinch of salt (optional)
  • 1 tsp balsamic vinegar
  • Fresh parsley (chopped)
  • 1 tbsp fresh mint (chopped)
  • A drizzle of olive oil or truffle oil (optional)

Step by step method

Step 1

. In a bowl, mix sliced mushrooms with olive oil, balsamic vinegar, thyme, salt, and pepper.
. Let them marinate for 15-20 minutes to soak up the flavors.

Step 2

.In a large pan, heat olive oil and butter.
.Sauté the onion until translucent, then add garlic and stir for 1 minute.
.Add the arborio rice and toast it in the pan for 2 minutes until slightly golden.
.Start adding the warm stock, one ladle at a time, stirring continuously until each ladleful is absorbed before adding the next.
Continue this process until the rice is creamy and tender (about 20 minutes).

Step 3

In a separate pan, sauté the marinated mushrooms over medium-high heat until browned and tender.

Step 4

. Stir the cooked mushrooms into the risotto.
.Add parmesan cheese, season with salt and pepper, and stir well.

Tips & variations

  • ✓ Use Arborio or Carnaroli Rice: These short-grain varieties release more starch, giving risotto its signature creamy texture.
  • ✓ Keep the Stock Warm: Always add warm stock to the risotto — cold stock will shock the rice and slow the cooking process.
  • ✓ Toast the Rice: Don’t skip toasting the rice in oil and butter for 1-2 minutes. It helps lock in flavor and gives a better texture.
  • ✓ Cook Mushrooms Separately: Always cook the marinated mushrooms in a separate pan before adding them to the risotto — it keeps their texture intact and enhances the flavor.
